### Critique Approach
```
🧐 Critique: [Approach/Component]

**Approach:** Separated encoders + early fusion for multimodal embeddings

**Strengths:**
1. ✅ Flexible - each encoder can be optimized independently
2. ✅ Modular - easy to swap out one encoder
3. ✅ Feasible - each encoder well-tested

**Weaknesses:**
1. ❌ Doesn't capture cross-modal interactions
   - Example: "Coffee shop" context comes from video (Starbucks logo) + location + IMU (sitting pattern)
   - Early fusion (concatenation) doesn't learn these interactions
   - Result: Embedding may not capture "work at Starbucks" concept

2. ❌ Requires fusion logic
   - How to weight each modality?
   - What if one modality is missing (no video in low light)?
   - Result: System complexity + potential for poor fusion

3. ❌ No learned representations
   - Early fusion = concatenation of pre-learned features
   - Doesn't learn joint representations
   - Result: May not be optimal for cross-modal tasks

**Missing Consideration:**
- **Cross-modal attention:** Could use attention mechanism to dynamically weight modalities
- **Late fusion with joint learning:** Could learn shared representation space
- **Multimodal contrastive learning:** Could learn relationships between modalities

**Alternatives:**

1. **Cross-Modal Attention**
   - Example: CLIP-style attention between vision and other modalities
   - Pros: Learns to attend to relevant modalities dynamically
   - Cons: More complex, needs training data
   
2. **Multimodal Transformer (LMM-based)**
   - Example: ImageBind (Meta), FLAVA
   - Pros: Learns joint representations across modalities
   - Cons: Larger model, slower on-device

3. **Late Fusion with Learned Projection**
   - Example: Project all modalities to shared space, then attention-fuse
   - Pros: Balances early/late fusion trade-offs
   - Cons: Needs projection layer training

**Recommendation:**
- Start with early fusion (simpler, faster to implement)
- If evaluation shows suboptimal performance, upgrade to cross-modal attention
- Don't over-commit to early fusion in thesis - frame as "initial approach"

**Data Needed:**
- Baseline performance: How does early fusion compare to late fusion on your tasks?
- Literature: Has anyone compared early vs late fusion for multimodal embeddings?
```

### Identify Risks
```
⚠️ Risk Assessment: [Component/Approach]

**Component:** Battery Optimization

**Claimed Performance:** <30% battery per day

**Risk Analysis:**

### 🟢 Low Risk (Probability < 30%)
- IMU + GPS: ~2-4%/day ✅
  - Already well-understood
  - Many devices handle this without issue
- Mitigation: Use existing sensor fusion libraries

### 🟡 Medium Risk (Probability 30-60%)
- Video + Audio: ~15-20%/day ⚠️
  - High variance based on device (iPhone vs Android)
  - Depends on sampling rate (every 5 min vs every 10 min)
- Mitigation:
  - Adaptive sampling (reduce frequency when battery < 20%)
  - Duty cycling (10s capture, then sleep for 5 min)
  
### 🔴 High Risk (Probability > 60%)
- Continuous background processing
  - Even when not capturing, privacy pipeline is "ready" → overhead
  - Depends on OS (iOS background limits vs Android)
- Mitigation:
  - Test on real devices (not simulator)
  - Use OS-specific optimizations (background tasks)
  - Consider iOS restrictions more than Android

### 🔴 Show-Stopper Risk (Probability > 20%)
- Privacy pipeline (face/text blur)
  - Running MediaPipe + OCR continuously is CPU-intensive
  - May consume 10-15% just for blur
  - Result: Leaving <15% for everything else
- Mitigation:
  - Only blur on-demand (when storing, not always)
  - Use GPU acceleration (if available)
  - Consider off-device blur (but contradicts privacy-by-design)

**Overall Risk Assessment:**
- **Most concerning:** Privacy pipeline battery overhead
- **Data needed:** Real device testing (not assumptions)
- **Probability of meeting <30% target:** 🟡 40-60%

**Recommendation:**
1. **Week 5:** Prototype on real device, measure battery
2. **If >30%:** Reconsider sampling strategy (reduce from every 5 min to every 10 min)
3. **If still >30%:** Move blur to cloud (but document privacy trade-off)
4. **Add buffer in timeline:** Allocate 1-2 extra weeks for optimization

**Alternatives if Battery Fails:**
1. **Reduced Scope:** Don't collect video, just IMU + location + audio keywords
2. **Triggered-Only Recording:** Only capture when user taps "record"
3. **Companion Device:** Use dedicated wearable (less processing overhead)
```

### Propose Alternatives
```
🔄 Alternative Approaches: [Current Approach]

**Current Approach:** Continuous life logging + automatic pattern detection

**Alternative 1: Incremental Personalization**
**Concept:** Don't collect all data at once. Start simple, add modalities gradually.

**Timeline:**
- Weeks 1-4: Text-only memories (from conversations)
- Weeks 5-8: + Location memories
- Weeks 9-12: + IMU/activity memories
- Weeks 13-16: + Vision memories
- Weeks 17-20: + Audio memories

**Pros:**
- ✅ Lower risk (if one modality fails, others still work)
- ✅ Iterative learning (can optimize each modality before adding more)
- ✅ User adaptation (users get used to system gradually)
- ✅ Lower battery/storage impact initially

**Cons:**
- ❌ Slower to reach full vision (only multimodal in last 4 weeks)
- ❌ May miss early multimodal patterns
- ❌ Doesn't demonstrate full potential until end

**Suitability:** Good for **conservative approach**, but conflicts with "full system" goal

---

**Alternative 2: Context-Aware Sampling**
**Concept:** Don't collect always. Collect only when context suggests value.

**Sampling Rules:**
- Work hours (9am-5pm): Collect every 5 min
- Commute (7-9am, 5-7pm): Collect every 10 min
- Home (evening): Collect every 30 min
- Sleep (11pm-7am): No collection

**Pros:**
- ✅ Massive battery savings (70-80% reduction in non-valuable times)
- ✅ Privacy-preserving (less data in intimate settings)
- ✅ Focus on high-value contexts (work, commuting)

**Cons:**
- ❌ May miss unexpected events
- ❌ Requires accurate context detection (chicken-egg problem)
- ❌ May create "gaps" that confuse pattern detection

**Suitability:** Good for **practical deployment**, but complexity high

---

**Alternative 3: User-Initiated Recording**
**Concept:** User controls when to record. Agent prompts user.

**Workflow:**
1. Agent: "I notice you're in a meeting. Want me to record?"
2. User: "Yes" → Record 5 min
3. Agent: "I noticed you're at a new restaurant. Want me to remember this?"
4. User: "No" → Don't record

**Pros:**
- ✅ Full user control (zero privacy concerns)
- ✅ No battery/storage issues (user decides when)
- ✅ Higher data quality (user only records when valuable)

**Cons:**
- ❌ Burden on user (constant prompts)
- ❌ Incomplete data (user forgets or declines)
- ❌ Doesn't demonstrate "continuous" aspect of thesis

**Suitability:** Good for **privacy-first approach**, but undermines "automatic" thesis goal

---

**Alternative 4: Hybrid: Selective Continuous** (RECOMMENDED)
**Concept:** Balance between continuous and selective.

**Strategy:**
- **Always-on (low cost):** IMU, location, basic activity
- **Periodic (medium cost):** Video/audio every 10 min for 30s
- **User-triggered (high value):** Manual "remember this" captures

**Pros:**
- ✅ Baseline data always available (IMU + location)
- ✅ Periodic captures some context (video/audio)
- ✅ User can add high-value moments
- ✅ Battery/storage balanced

**Cons:**
- ❌ Still complex to implement
- ❌ May miss events between periodic captures

**Suitability:** **BEST COMPROMISE** between all approaches

**Recommendation:** 
- Implement Alternative 4 as primary approach
- Design fallback to Alternative 1 (incremental) if Alternative 4 fails
- Test Alternative 2 (context-aware) in pilot study

**Timeline Adjustment Needed:**
- If Alternative 4: +1 week (hybrid logic)
- If need fallback: +2-3 weeks (incremental mode)
- Total buffer: +3-4 weeks → Extend to 23-24 weeks
```
